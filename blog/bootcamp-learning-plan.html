My Learning Plan for Bootcamp

ðŸ’¡What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My long term goal is to become a well-rounded developer who can work across different areas of software development.
I'm not focused only on front-end or back-end -- I want to explore both and find what suits me best as I grow.
I hope to work in a supportive team where I can keep learning, contribute meaningfully, and gain real-world experience.
Eventually, I'd like to be part of projects that solve real problems and have a positive impact on people's lives.
I'm also open to teaching or mentoring in the future, so I can support others starting their own coding journey.

ðŸ’¡What are you hoping to use the skills you gain during this course for?
What impact would you like to make once you leave Dev Academy? Do you have a destination in mind?

I want to use the skills I gain at Dev Academy to start a new career in tech -- one that's more stable, creative, and meaningful to me.
I've worked in hospitality for years, and I know how important it is to work hard and connect with people.
Now I want to build something with those same values, but in a different way -- through code.
I don't have one fixed destination yet, but I'm open to exploring different roles and growing my skills step by step.
Eventually, I want to be someone who can confidently contribute to a team, solve real-world problems with tech, and maybe even build something of my own one day.

ðŸ’¡What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

One of my biggest strengths is that I'm meticulous and committed--I take time to understand things deeply, and I don't give up easily.
I've also become more reflective and self-aware through Foundations, which I think will help me handle stress and grow during Bootcamp.
But I know that my biggest limitation might be working under time pressure.
Since I tend to learn slowly and carefully, I sometimes need more time than others.
Another challenge might be expressing myself in English, especially in group discussions.
But I'm learning to be okay with making mistakes, and I'm trying to be more confident in asking for help when I need it.

ðŸ’¡What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I think my biggest non-technical challenge will be staying focused and keeping up with the pace of Bootcamp.
Since I learn more slowly than others, I often feel pressure to catch up or compare myself--which can make it hard to concentrate.
Working in groups might also be challenging because I'm still building confidence in communicating in English, especially when I'm unsure about something.
But I'm planning to manage this by being honest about how I'm doing, asking for support early, and reminding myself that learning at my own pace is okay.

I want to develop better communication skills--both speaking and listening-- so I can collaborate more openly and trust myself more.
I also want to get better at receiving feedback without taking it personally, and giving helpful feedback to others in a kind and respectful way.
Most of all, I want to build emotional resilience, so I can stay calm and focused even when things feel difficult or overwhelming.

I expect myself to stay open--to learning, to challenges, and to support.

Most of all, I want to remind myself that it's okay not to be perfect--I'm here to learn, and that means being brave enough to make mistakes.

ðŸ’¡How and where will you seek help in a timely way?

I'll try to ask for help as soon as I feel stuck for more than 20-30 minutes, instead of trying to push through it alone for too long.
I'll use the group chat, ask my peers, or reach out to facilitators when I need support--especially if I feel confused or overwhelmed.
I know that asking for help isn't a sign of weakness, but a smart and healthy way to learn.
I'll also keep using journaling and reflection to track what I'm struggling with, so I can be clearer when I ask questions.
My goal is to speak up earlier, not later--and trust that I don't have to go through it all alone.
